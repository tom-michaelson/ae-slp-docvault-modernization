***********************************************************************
*    Deferred Interest Calculator
***********************************************************************
* This COBOL program implements a Deferred Interest Calculator. It's designed to calculate the financial outcome of a
* deferred interest promotion on a purchase. Here's a breakdown of its key components and calculations:
*
* Structure:
* The program is named DEFERRED-INTEREST-CALC.
* It's divided into main sections: WORKING-STORAGE SECTION for variable declarations and
* PROCEDURE DIVISION for the program logic.
*
* Key Variables:
*   PCH-AMT: Purchase amount
*   PROMO-PERIOD-MONTHS: Promotional period duration (default 12 months)
*   POST-PROMO-APR: Annual Percentage Rate after the promotion (default 18%)
*   AMOUNT-PAID: Total amount paid during the promotional period
*   REMAINING-BALANCE: Unpaid balance after the promotional period
*   RET-INTEREST: Retroactive interest charged if not paid in full
*   TOTAL-DUE: Total amount due after the promotion
*
* Main Procedures:
*   GET-USER-INPUT: Collects user input for purchase amount, promotional period, APR, and amount paid.
*   CALCULATE-BALANCE: Performs the main calculations.
*   DISPLAY-RESULTS: Outputs the results.
*
* Calculations:
*   a. Remaining Balance: REMAINING-BALANCE = PCH-AMT - AMOUNT-PAID
*   b. Retroactive Interest (if balance not paid in full): RET-INTEREST = PCH-AMT * (POST-PROMO-APR / 100)
*       This calculates the interest on the entire purchase amount for one year.
*   c. Total Due (if balance not paid in full): TOTAL-DUE = REMAINING-BALANCE + RET-INTEREST
*
* Key Logic:
*   If the remaining balance is greater than zero after the promotional period:
*   It calculates retroactive interest on the full purchase amount.
*   The total due is the sum of the remaining balance and the retroactive interest.
*   If the balance is paid in full (remaining balance <= 0):
*   No interest is charged.
*
*   The total due is set to the remaining balance (which may be negative if overpaid).
*
* Output:
*   The program displays the purchase amount, amount paid, remaining balance, and if applicable, the deferred
*   interest charged and the total amount due.
*
* This calculator demonstrates how deferred interest promotions work: if the full purchase amount isn't paid by the
* end of the promotional period, interest is charged retroactively on the entire original purchase amount, not just
* the remaining balance. This can result in a significant additional cost if the balance isn't paid in full during
* the promotional period.
******************************************************************
